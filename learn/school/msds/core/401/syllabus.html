MSDS 401: Introduction to Statistical Analysis
Instructor: Roy L. Sanford, Ph.D

Description: This course teaches students the fundamentals of conducting statistical analyses. This includes interpreting and evaluating statistical information. Topics covered include descriptive statistics, central tendency, dispersion, data display, exploratory data analysis; probability; binomial and normal distributions; inference, including confidence intervals and hypothesis testing; correlation; multiple linear regression; contingency tables and chi-square. Selected contemporary statistical concepts are introduced to supplement traditional statistical methods.

- perform statistical analyses,

- interpret statistical information,

- evaluate statistical information,

- and use the language R for data analysis.
